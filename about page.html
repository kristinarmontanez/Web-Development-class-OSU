Meet the Pups!

Things we bring on hikes:

  • Poop bags! Hikers appreciate not having dog poop on the trail.
  • Both dog's ID collars, with a phone number to reach me at.
  • Large water bottle and an empty collapsable water bowl for when the pups get thirsty.
  • Some of their dog food and treats, which help if there are distractions on the trial.
  • A small first-aid kit that includes "liquid bandaids" for dogs.
  • Dog insect repellant. No one likes deer ticks!

About Us

We live in the beautiful state of Washington, where we enjoy hiking opportunities just about anywhere we go! First, meet Luna. She’s a husky-lab-rotty mix. She’s often grumpy but is always in a good mood when we spend the day outdoors. The little white husky is Inlé. She is spunky, loving, and has very strong opinions on…well…just about everything. Their favorite hiking spots include any place that they can possibly find treasures to sniff out and carry home with them. I have an ever-growing pile of "fetch sticks" brought home that serve as trophies of hikes past.

During our hikes, we have encountered many types of wildlife, including black bears, mountain lions, moose and elk. To be fair, we lived in Alaska for a time, and it’s hard to go outside without passing a moose at some point. When hiking in the wilderness, make sure to always tell someone where you are going and your estimated time of return. If you are able, protect yourself and your fur babies by keeping to the designated trail, hiking only during daylight, and always keep a form of protection with you such as pepper spray to ward off bears and larger animals. And even if you are in comfortable temperatures, it’s always wise to bring extra layers of clothing just in case! If you love hiking as much as we do, please check out the Visit Here page for places that are worth exploring.

If you are interested in hiking around the Seattle area, download The Seattle Backcountry Hiking Guide.

The information in this guide was compiled by Craig Romano, an award winning author and co-author of more than 20 guidebooks on hiking in Washington State and the Pacific Northwest.

My Preferred Accessories

First, you're going to want to get yourself a good water container. For yourself, and if you are a fur-baby parent, for your pups too. I would suggest something that is metal over plastic incase you drop it. Metal containers also keep cold longer. The water container I like is the Yeti Rambler. It's easily portable and easy to open.
